About our lab

Pioneering air quality science

The Air Quality and Aerosol Management (AQAR) Laboratory is dedicated to understanding the physical, chemical, and optical properties of atmospheric aerosols — and what they mean for human health and climate.

We bridge fundamental aerosol science and real-world air quality management, turning careful measurements into research that informs environmental policy and public health decisions.

  • State-of-the-art instrumentation

    Advanced instruments for aerosol characterization and real-time monitoring.

  • Field measurement campaigns

    Sampling across diverse environments, from city streets to remote sites.

  • Computational modeling

    Models for air quality prediction, source apportionment, and policy support.

Thirteen active directions across monitoring, characterization, exposure modelling, and emerging pollutants.

Indoor and outdoor monitoring to characterize pollutant levels across micro-environments.

Air quality in metro micro-environments and its implications for commuter and worker health.

Physical and chemical characterization of PM10 and PM2.5: size, composition, and morphology.

PM2.5PM10

MicroplasticsBlack Carbon

Human Health Risk Modelling

Respiratory-tract mass deposition and exposure pathway modelling for cancer and non-cancer risk.

HRT ModelRisk Assessment

Bioaerosol Research
