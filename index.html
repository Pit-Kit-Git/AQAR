About Our Lab

Pioneering Air Quality Science

The Air Quality and Aerosol Management (AQAR) Laboratory is dedicated to understanding the physical, chemical, and optical properties of atmospheric aerosols and their impact on human health and climate.

Founded with a mission to bridge the gap between fundamental aerosol science and real-world air quality management, our lab conducts innovative research that directly informs environmental policy and public health decisions.

State-of-the-Art Instrumentation

Advanced instruments for aerosol characterization and real-time monitoring

Field Measurement Campaigns

Measurements across diverse environments, from urban to remote areas

Computational Modeling

Advanced models for air quality prediction and policy support

What We Study

Research Focus Areas

Active research directions across monitoring, characterization, exposure modelling, and emerging pollutants

Air Quality Monitoring

Indoor and outdoor/atmospheric air quality monitoring to characterize pollutant levels across micro-environments.

Indoor AirAmbient Monitoring

Subway Air Quality & Human Health

Air quality in subway/metro transportation micro-environments and its implications for commuter and worker health.

Metro TransitCommuter Exposure

Particulate Matter Characterization

Physical and chemical characterization of PM10 and PM2.5, including size distribution, composition, and morphology.

PM2.5PM10

Emerging Air Pollutants

Investigating microplastics and black carbon as emerging airborne contaminants and their atmospheric behaviour.

MicroplasticsBlack Carbon

Human Health Risk Modelling

HRT (human respiratory tract) mass deposition and exposure pathway modelling for cancer and non-cancer risk assessment.

HRT ModelRisk Assessment

Bioaerosol Research

Bioaerosols in indoor and subway micro-environments, assessed through Quantitative Microbial Risk Assessment (QMRA).

BioaerosolsQMRA

Natural & Anthropogenic Sources

Distinguishing natural and human-driven pollution sources and evaluating their respective impacts on human health.

Source TypesHealth Impact

Air Pollution Modelling

Source-based and receptor-based air pollution modelling to link emissions with observed ambient concentrations.

Source ModellingReceptor Modelling

Transboundary Pollution

Tracing transboundary sources of air pollution and their environmental and human health risks across regions.

Long-range TransportRegional Risk

Source Apportionment

Identifying and quantifying contributions of emission sources to ambient particulate matter using receptor modelling.

PMFReceptor Modelling

Emission Inventory

Compiling and quantifying pollutant emission inventories across source categories to support air quality management.

Emission InventorySource Data

Microplastics & Toxic Metal Interactions

Studying how microplastics interact with toxic metals in the environment, and the combined risks this poses.

MicroplasticsToxic Metals

Biochar: Production & Application

Producing and applying biochar from agricultural, forest, and campus waste as a sustainable waste management strategy.

BiocharWaste Valorisation
