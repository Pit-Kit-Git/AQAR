AQAR Lab

Air Quality and Aerosol Research

We trace how air pollutants and aerosols move through indoor rooms, subway platforms, and transboundary air masses — and translate that into measurable risk for human health and ecosystems. From dispersion modeling to bioaerosol monitoring, our work spans the full path from source to exposure.

Research

What we work on

Ten connected lines of work, from modeling how pollutants disperse to assessing what they cost human health and the environment.

APM

Air Pollution Modeling

Dispersion and receptor modeling of air pollutants across urban and regional scales.

AQAC

Indoor & Outdoor Air Quality

Assessment and characterization of air quality across indoor and outdoor environments.

NTAP

Transboundary Air Pollutants

Natural and transboundary air pollutants and their long-range atmospheric transport.

EAP

Emerging Air Pollutants

Airborne microplastics and black carbon as emerging contaminants of concern.

SUBWAY

Subway Air Quality

Air quality in underground subway transportation systems.

HHR

Human Health Risks

Health risks in subway systems and indoor houses from pollutant exposure.

BIO‑QMRA

Bioaerosol Monitoring

Bioaerosol monitoring and quantitative microbial risk assessment (probability of infection).

HRT

Health Risk Models

Mass deposition and exposure pathway modeling for human respiratory tracts.

ERA

Ecological Risk Assessment

Assessing pollutant risk to ecosystems, beyond human health impacts alone.

W2SP

Waste to Sustainable Product

Solid waste management pathways that convert waste into sustainable products.
