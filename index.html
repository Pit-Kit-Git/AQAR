Infrastructure

Lab facilities

Fine Particulate Sampler (MFC)

Mass-Flow-Controlled sampler that pulls ambient air through a size-selective inlet; heavier dust drops by inertia while PM2.5/PM10 is trapped on a filter for lab analysis.

PM2.5PM10MFC

Fine Particulate Sampler (MMFC)

Microprocessor Mass Flow Control version that auto-corrects for real-time temperature and pressure changes for absolute flow accuracy while trapping PM2.5/PM10.

MMFCAuto-compensated

Personal Air Sampler

Wearable, precision-calibrated pump that continuously pulls breathing-zone air through a filter to assess individual exposure to PM and gaseous hazards.

WearablePersonal Exposure

Handy Air Sampler (Gaseous)

Calibrated rotameter draws ambient air through glass impinger tubes with absorbing solutions, trapping SO2 and NOx for quantitative analysis.

SO2NOxImpinger

Aethalometer

Real-time, continuous black carbon (soot) monitoring via light attenuation at multiple wavelengths; distinguishes fossil-fuel from biomass-burning sources.

Black CarbonMulti-wavelength

Indoor Air Quality Monitor

Multi-sensor device for real-time AQI, PM2.5, PM10, particle count, CO2, HCHO, TVOC, CO, O3, temperature and humidity.

AQICO2/TVOCPM

Outdoor Air Quality Monitor

Pole-mounted station for long-term outdoor AQI, PM1/PM2.5/PM10, gaseous pollutants, and meteorological data (wind speed and direction).

OutdoorMet data

Muffle Furnace

Heats samples to ~1200 C to burn off organic fractions via thermal oxidation for ash and elemental/organic carbon determination.

1200 COC/EC

Incubator

The Air Quality and Aerosol Research (AQAR) Laboratory at IISER Kolkata studies the physical, chemical, and biological nature of atmospheric particulate matter and the risks it carries, from carcinogenic trace metals and black carbon to airborne microplastics and bioaerosols.

Our work couples rigorous field measurement with receptor and dispersion modelling (PMF, CMB, AERMOD) and quantitative health-risk assessment, producing evidence that directly informs environmental policy and public health decisions.

From subway platforms to remote rooftops, we characterise the air people actually breathe and translate that science into cleaner-air solutions for communities and policymakers.
